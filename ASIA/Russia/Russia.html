Russia

Capital: Moscow

Population: (2021 est.) 143,185,000

Language: Russian

Currency: Russian ruble

Russia, or the Russian Federation, is a country spanning Eastern Europe and Northern Asia. It is the largest country in the world, covering over 17,125,191 square kilometres, and encompassing one-eighth of Earth's inhabitable landmass.

There is no place like Russia when speaking of architecture. The famous onion domes, red bricks, colourful facades and Byzantine-like style make the Russian architecture very distinctive and above all – beautiful. And while there are many common features, the architecture of each city has its own characteristics. The Red Square and Kremlin with its red bricks along with the colourful and unique St Basil’s Cathedral are breathtaking. The golden-green combinations of colours and eclectic style in Saint Petersburg exude luxury and power. Different parts of Russia were under different influences, some reflecting Muslim features, some resembling eastern Asia look. However, one style that pervades in the whole of Russia is the Soviet Architecture, a significant style of modern Russian history.
